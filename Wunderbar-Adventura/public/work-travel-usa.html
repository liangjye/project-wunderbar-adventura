Work & Travel USA

In recent years, Work & Travel USA (J1 Summer Work/Travel)has been popularized as the trend of traveling overseas emerged among the thought of youngsters , and it may be the most rewarding experience for a youth's university life. Spend your university/college break by experiencing a lifetime adventure in the United States of America. You will be able to experience life in the bustling metropolitan cities- NYC, Boston, San Francisco, etc, to the breath-taking national parks- Yellowstone, Yosemite, Grand Canyon, etc, to the sunny beaches- Miami, Santa Monica, etc. Join us now, and you will have an unforgettable experience!

Eligibility Criteria

It is crucial for us to confirm your eligibility before accepting you into the program to ensure an adequate program participation. In favor of joining Work & Travel USA, you are required to:

1. Age between 18 to 28 years old
2. A full-time university/college student OR a graduate no more than 6 months prior to the start of your program
3. Have an average English proficiency
4. Be able to fulfil minimum 2.5 months’ work commitment in the United States

Program Duration 

The J-1 Work & Travel USA program lasts a maximum work duration of 4 months, plus a grace period of 30 days to travel around United States after your work commitment.

Your program dates depend on your university/college semester break that are discussed between you and the employer before starting the program. Participants mostly work for 3 to 3.5 months as they are required to return home when classes begin.

Program Intake & Seasons
J-1 Work & Travel USA applications are accepted twice a year. You can choose your intake, depending on your date of availability.

Winter
The nighttime tend to be longer than usual. This particular season will present you the festivals that are rarely celebrated in our country but you can always see the festivals on TV or the internet! Thanksgiving! Black Friday (commercialized)! Christmas! and New Year Celebration should not be miss for this season! The falling snow may be the most natural decorative that you must witness!

Summer
It may be the same temperature just like our home country. However, you will witness one of the clearest blue sky in your lifetime because of the absence of clouds. With the low humidity over the States comparing to us, you will not feel the stuffiness like you used to feel in Malaysia but instead some slight sensation on burning when you feel the sunlight.

Program Location & Considerations
With the vast land of The United States, from state A to Z, we have 50 different states to choose from and it's up to your decision! You could be working in the city that never sleeps NYC, in the mid-West, or even sunny beaches in Florida. Every US state hides gems that are waiting to be discovered. The most important thing is to keep an open mind, and go boldly wherever you feel like it! The two most important considerations when choosing a location are:

Area
Most job locations are located in tourist filled areas (that's where you come in to save their day). If you prefer a fast-paced and town-like lifestyle, you can consider opportunities offered in the big cities like Atlanta or New York City; if you prefer a relaxing and laid-back lifestyle, you should choose a remote, nature-based location like National Parks. However, do keep in mind that living in a city can be quite costly, while living in remote areas can be far away from hustle and bustle you are expecting.

Climate
If you not a big fan of the cold, you should look at opportunities offered in the southern part of the country; if you are not fond with the heat, you should look at opportunities offered in the northern part of the country. Be sure to Google the climate in your area which coincides with your program dates to be fully prepared for the weather!
Salary & Employment Benefits
You will be earning exactly what Americans would receive. Most employers pay around $8 to $13 per hour depending on job nature and location. Full time work is 40hrs/week, and overtime pay is usually 1.5 time the normal rate. You are allowed to seek a second job in your free time to earn extra bucks! Overall, you can very much expect to earn enough to cover your initial costs and a major part of your expenses in the United States.
